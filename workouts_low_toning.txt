
Low-Intensity Toning Workout
Total Time: 20 Minutes
Objective: Gradually improve muscle definition, strength, and flexibility with low-impact exercises.
________________________________________
Warm-Up (3 Minutes)
1.	Deep Breathing with Arm Raises: 1 minute
    o	Inhale as you lift your arms overhead, exhale as you lower them.
2.	Marching in Place: 1 minute
    o	Lift knees gently, swinging arms naturally.
3.	Neck Rolls and Shoulder Rolls: 1 minute
________________________________________
Main Workout (15 Minutes, Circuit Style)
Perform each exercise for 40 seconds, rest for 20 seconds. Complete 2 rounds.
1.	Chair Pose (Static Hold)
    o	Sit back as if into a chair, keeping arms raised and back straight. Hold the position.
    o	Engages the thighs, glutes, and core.
2.	Modified Push-Ups (Knees)
    o	Perform push-ups with knees on the ground.
    o	Gently strengthens the chest, shoulders, and triceps.
3.	Standing Side Leg Lifts
    o	Lift one leg out to the side, keeping it straight. Alternate sides.
    o	Tones the outer thighs and improves balance.
4.	Seated Torso Twists
    o	Sit on a chair or the floor, twisting gently side to side.
    o	Engages the obliques and core.
5.	Glute Bridge
    o	Lie on your back, feet flat on the ground, and lift hips toward the ceiling.
    o	Tones the glutes and strengthens the lower back.
6.	Cat-Cow Stretch
    o	On all fours, alternate between arching and rounding your back.
    o	Gently stretches and strengthens the core and spine.
7.	Wall Push-Ups
    o	Push against a wall at an angle, keeping your body straight.
    o	Tones the arms and chest with minimal impact.
8.	Clamshells
    o	Lie on your side with knees bent, and open your top leg like a clamshell. Switch sides after 20 seconds.
    o	Strengthens the glutes and hips.
________________________________________
Cool Down (2 Minutes)
1.	Hamstring Stretch: 1 minute
2.	Seated Forward Fold: 1 minute
________________________________________
This low-intensity toning workout is ideal for beginners or those recovering from high-intensity training. It focuses on controlled movements to build strength and muscle definition without strain.
