Low-Intensity Weight Loss Workout
Total Time: 20 Minutes
Objective: Support gradual weight loss, improve flexibility, and build foundational fitness through gentle movements.
________________________________________
Warm-Up (3 Minutes)
1.	Deep Breathing with Arm Raises: 1 minute
    o	Inhale while raising arms overhead; exhale while lowering them.
2.	Marching in Place: 1 minute
    o	Lift knees gently while swinging arms.
3.	Neck Rolls and Shoulder Rolls: 1 minute
________________________________________
Main Workout (15 Minutes)
Perform each exercise for 40 seconds, rest for 20 seconds. Complete 2 rounds.
1.	Slow Bodyweight Squats
    o	Lower yourself into a squat slowly, ensuring knees don’t go past toes.
    o	Builds lower body strength with controlled movement.
2.	Modified Knee Push-Ups
    o	Perform push-ups with knees on the ground.
    o	Strengthens the chest and arms gently.
3.	Seated Torso Twists
    o	Sit on a chair or the floor, twisting your torso side to side.
    o	Engages the core without strain.
4.	Standing Side Leg Raises
    o	Lift one leg to the side while balancing on the other. Alternate sides.
    o	Improves balance and strengthens hips.
5.	Step Touches
    o	Step side-to-side while gently swinging your arms.
    o	Light cardio for full-body movement.
6.	Wall Push-Ups
    o	Push against a wall at an angle, keeping your body straight.
    o	Builds upper body strength with minimal impact.
7.	Cat-Cow Pose
    o	On all fours, alternate between arching your back (cow) and rounding it (cat).
    o	Improves flexibility and gently stretches the spine.
8.	Standing Forward Stretch
    o	Slowly bend forward to touch your toes or as far as comfortable.
    o	Stretches the hamstrings and improves circulation.
________________________________________
Cool Down (2 Minutes)
1.	Child’s Pose: 1 minute
    o	Sit back on your heels, stretching arms forward.
2.	Deep Breathing in Seated Position: 1 minute
________________________________________
This low-intensity workout promotes gradual calorie burn, improves circulation, and supports joint health, making it ideal for beginners or those seeking a gentler approach to weight loss.
